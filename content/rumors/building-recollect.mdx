Recollect was my first project as a fullstack dev at Timeless. Three years of code, no docs, no comments, files long enough that you'd lose your place scrolling. I read through it on day one and was pretty sure I was cooked.

The plan, such as it was: don't break anything obvious, learn the shape of the app by fixing the small stuff first, and earn the right to touch the scary parts. That's how it went. A nudge here, a refactor there, a feature once the surrounding code stopped fighting me. Months of that. Slowly the codebase got friendlier and the app got faster.

By the time we hit launch I'd talked myself into expecting a quiet day. Maybe a small ripple online, maybe a handful of signups. We'd ship, we'd watch the dashboards, we'd move on to the next thing.

It blew up. Not rocketship blew up, but the kind of traction where you keep refreshing the chart because you don't quite believe the number. Real users, real usage, the support inbox actually filling up. The dashboards we'd set up half-jokingly turned into something we watched all afternoon.

I learned more from the months before launch than I did from the launch itself. But the launch is the part I tell people about, because it's the part that reminded me why I started doing this in the first place.
