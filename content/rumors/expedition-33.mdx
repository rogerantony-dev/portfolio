I picked up Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 the way you pick up any other game on a Tuesday. Half-attention, casual install, low expectations. Forty minutes in I was sitting up. A few hours in I'd quietly cancelled the rest of my evening.

I haven't been pulled into a game like that in years. The story, the combat, the world, all of it just clicked, and somewhere in the middle it hit that kid-on-Christmas-morning nerve I'd honestly forgotten video games could still hit.

Then the ending happened. I'm a grown ass man and I sat there with actual tears in my eyes when the credits rolled. That's the post. Go play it.
